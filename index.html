Material Work

AS Vinayaga Construction is renowned for its high-quality residential projects, with a strong emphasis on selecting durable and sustainable building materials. Their approach ensures that every home built meets the highest standards of safety, comfort, and aesthetics.
1. Foundation Materials
Cement: Premium-grade cement (e.g., OPC 53/43 or PPC) is used to ensure a solid and durable foundation. Sand: Well-graded river sand or M-sand (manufactured sand) is used for mixing concrete and plastering. Aggregates: Crushed stones or gravel of varying sizes are used for concrete mixtures, ensuring high structural integrity.
2. Structural Framework
Steel Reinforcement: High-quality TMT (Thermo-Mechanically Treated) bars are used for reinforced concrete structures to enhance strength and flexibility. Concrete: Ready-Mix Concrete (RMC) or site-mixed concrete is used based on project requirements to provide consistent quality.
3. 3. Wall Construction
Bricks/Blocks: AS Vinayaga uses red clay bricks, fly ash bricks, or AAC (Autoclaved Aerated Concrete) blocks, depending on project specifications and client preferences. AAC blocks are lightweight, eco-friendly, and thermally efficient. Mortar: A carefully prepared cement-sand mix ensures strong bonding between bricks or blocks.
4. Roofing Materials
Concrete Slabs: RCC slabs with steel reinforcement ensure long-lasting and robust roofing solutions. Waterproofing: High-performance waterproofing membranes and sealants are used to prevent leakage and dampness.
5. Flooring
Tiles: Premium vitrified tiles, ceramic tiles, or natural stones like marble and granite are selected based on design and budget. Skirting: Matching skirting tiles or stones provide a seamless finish to flooring.
6. Plastering and Finishing
Plastering: Cement and sand are mixed in accurate proportions for internal and external wall plastering. Putty: High-quality wall putty is applied to achieve smooth surfaces before painting. Paints: AS Vinayaga uses weather-resistant exterior paints and washable interior emulsions to enhance durability and aesthetics.
7. Doors and Windows
Woodwork: Durable and termite-resistant hardwood or engineered wood is used for doors and frames. Windows: UPVC or aluminum-framed windows with glass panes ensure modern aesthetics and insulation.
8. Plumbing and Electrical
Plumbing: PVC, CPVC, or PPR pipes are used for water supply and drainage, ensuring leak-proof systems. Electrical Wiring: ISI-marked copper wires, modular switches, and energy-efficient fittings ensure safe and reliable electrical installations.
9. Roof and Wall Insulation
Heat Insulation: Materials like reflective sheets or thermal blocks are used to maintain energy efficiency. Moisture Protection: Waterproof coatings and sealants are applied to walls and roofs to prevent dampness and seepage.
10. Eco-Friendly Practices
Waste Management: Sustainable practices are followed to minimize material wastage during construction. Green Materials: Where possible, eco-friendly materials like fly ash bricks and energy-efficient glass are prioritized.
11. Additional Features
False Ceilings: Gypsum boards or POP (Plaster of Paris) ceilings for modern interiors. Rainwater Harvesting: Materials like HDPE tanks and filtration units are used for effective water conservation systems. Solar Integration: Solar panels and inverters are incorporated upon client request.
